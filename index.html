Commercial Deal Matrix

A simple filter for the right conversation.

Max is selective because the work is advisory. The best fit is a commercial situation where the property, borrower, owner, investor, or referral partner has enough substance for him to add real value.

Strong fit

  • Retail properties, gas stations, convenience stores, and strip centers
  • Investor-owned commercial properties that need leasing or management
  • Loan packages that need to be organized before going to banks
  • Transactions near seven figures or above, where the advisory work is justified
  • Referral partners with clear context and a serious client need

Not the first priority

  • Small residential searches that do not need commercial advisory work
  • Unqualified leads without ownership, borrower, tenant, or investor context
  • Requests that lack basic project details, capacity needs, or intended use
  • Deals where Max cannot help at the level the client deserves

Helpful details to bring

  • Property address, asset type, current use, and market area
  • Owner, borrower, investor, tenant, or referral role
  • Estimated value, lease status, financing need, and timing
  • For specialty uses like data centers: power, equipment, capacity, and operating requirements
